Being a husband, a dad and a programmer is a good mix of fulfilling and demanding.

It has been a constant balancing act for me.

I make coffee and breakfast while hanging out with our baby and try to sneak my wife an extra nap in the morning because she tries to sneak me a full night's sleep during the workweek.

I sneak read technology news feeds and Twitter before I give our baby a bath every night.

I write while I sit in the nursery stroking our baby's forehead to help her down to sleep for the night.

I will say that working remotely has been one of the best decisions I have made. I need all of the time I can get.

I get to stick around a lot longer in the morning. I get to sneak out of my office and say hi to my wife and kid throughout the day. I get to steal kisses and I don't need to miss any big developments or milestones.

If I am on call or fielding any issues or behind on a deadline, it isn't as bad because I am still here.

Even with this flexibility, I feel a lot of pressure to really push my boundaries and abilities. I need to constantly think about whether I am using my time wisely. I definitely struggle to optimize my time every single day in a field where being relevant means always learning.

Even when I'm not working, I am often thinking about work and what I could be doing better. I think about what I should be learning and writing about. I think about how I should be networking better and getting out of the house.

I have always wished that I could just eliminate sleep entirely. It feels like I could do so much more if I could but seeing as I can't, it's very important to get proper sleep every night so that I have the patience for this stuff every day.

It is difficult to justify time for much of anything outside of work and family time with a young baby, but it is also important to not allow exercise and fitness to fall to the wayside. I bought a bike desk to get my cardio in during the work day. I try not to pedal much during meetings because I wouldn't want to distract people with heavy breathing.

Sleep and exercise are incredibly important for maintaining the good condition of your body and brain, which I really need for all three of the roles I play.

Something that has been great for me lately is to take a day per week where I try to shun programming and professional development from my mind. It is incredibly recharging and lets me focus purely on family and to return somewhat refreshed from the previous week. I don’t always get this day in but I am finding it increasingly important to avoid burnout.

I hope that I can continue to find ways to optimize my life for the things that make me truly happy. I hope that I can continue to rise in all three of my roles. I want to be a little bit better at being a husband-dad-programmer every day.
